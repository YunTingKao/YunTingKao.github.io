Correct

Blue light (460-480 nm) suppresses melatonin. Melatonin is a hormone secreted by the enigmatic pineal gland in response to darkness. It modulates our sleep and wake patterns (Shatkin). Blue light in the evening would trick our brains to think it’s daytime, which inhibits the production of melatonin and decreases the quantity and quality of our sleep. Hence, minimizing the exposure to blue light could help prevent melatonin loss and fall asleep easier at the desire time.


References

Shatkin, Jess. Circadian Rhythms. 2021. PowerPoint Presentation

Gunnars, Kris. “How Blocking Blue Light at Night Helps You Sleep.” Healthline, Healthline Media, 21 May 2020, https://www.healthline.com/nutrition/block-blue-light-to-sleep-better#tinted-glasses.

Incorrect

Alcohol increases NREM and reduces REM and Stage 1 in the first sleep cycles.

  • Within a sleep cycle or two, the effects of alcohol are wearing off and “withdrawal” symptoms then occur:
    • Shallow sleep with multiple awakenings (Stage 1 rebound)
    • REM rebound (often associated with vivid dreams, sweating, and nightmares)
  • As a result, drinking more than 1 drink before bed may result in Excessive daytime sleepiness (EDS)
  • Acts as a muscle relaxant and can exacerbate snoring and sleep apnea
  • Alcohol 6 hours before bed may fragment sleep
Overall, alcohol decreases the sleep quality.


References

Shatkin, Jess, and Argelinda Baroni. Sleep Hygiene. 2021. PowerPoint Presentation

Incorrect

  • Nicotine is thought to exert its strongest effects on sleep architecture at the beginning of sleep but then exert effects of nicotine withdrawal later in sleep epoch.
  • Dose dependent
    • Lower doses tend to stimulate REM
    • Higher doses suppress REM
  • Rebound upon discontinuation increases wake time and decreases Stage 3
  • Withdrawal begins 6 – 12 hours after last use and can last 3 weeks
Overall, nicotine disrupts sleep quality and causes individuals to wake at night.


References

Shatkin, Jess, and Argelinda Baroni. Sleep Hygiene. 2021. PowerPoint Presentation
